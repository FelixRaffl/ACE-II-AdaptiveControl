\section{Conclusion}

The first-order self-tuning regulator from the reader runs on the ESP32 rig
with the encoder scaling fixed by the one-revolution test at \(44\) counts per
revolution. The same derivation carried to the second-order plant identifies
all four parameters in simulation at \(T=\SI{2}{ms}\). At the hardware sampling
time of \(T=\SI{80}{ms}\), the second-order plant is structurally not
identifiable, which is why the second-order design stays a simulation result.
